## Notes

### Architecture Decisions

1. **"Blocked Drains" vs "Drain Unblocking" kept separate.** Despite semantic overlap, UK search data shows these as distinct queries. "Blocked drains" targets the problem ("I have blocked drains"), "drain unblocking" targets the solution ("I need drain unblocking"). Content will be differentiated accordingly.

2. **"Blocked Drains" elevated to core, "Drain Unblocking" as child.** "Blocked drains" has higher search volume and more commercial intent in UK local search.

3. **General Plumbing category has one core service (Bathroom Plumbing).** Bathroom plumbing is the highest-volume, highest-value service in this category. Kitchen Plumbing, Tap Repair, Shower Installation, and Pipe Replacement are all child pages.

4. **No separate service area pages.** The business serves Hinckley and surrounding villages. At this stage, all pages target "Hinckley" as the primary location. Service area expansion to individual town pages can be added later as a Phase 10 if needed.

5. **UK English throughout.** All content uses UK spelling (specialised, organisation, colour, neighbourhood). Phone numbers in UK format (0333 prefix). Postcodes not ZIP codes. County (Leicestershire) instead of state.

6. **24/7 messaging is a primary differentiator.** The emergency availability angle should be woven into every page, particularly Emergency Plumbing category and its children. The CTA "Call 0333 240 8428" should be prominent on every page.

7. **Lead-matching model language.** The business connects customers with vetted plumbers rather than employing plumbers directly. Content should say "our network of vetted plumbers" or "we connect you with qualified local plumbers" rather than "our plumbers" or "we'll come to your home."
